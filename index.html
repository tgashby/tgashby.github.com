Micron (2017-Present)

I work on the PhotoApps team which started in 2017 as two engineers and has grown to six in the last year. PhotoApps is a data collection and visualization platform for the photolithography stage of wafer development.
Within the first few months we were able to rearchitect the legacy application (PHP, Perl, MySQL) to reduce incident reports by 98% and increase application uptime and availability by over 500X.
For the last several months I have been leading the team in a migration / remake of the application in a new platform (Typescript (Angular 8+), RestFul apis in C# (.NET Core) and Django, and Azure Cloud Services for Sql, storage, and security).

Due to the security constraints around Micron IP, I am unable to get any screenshots out. :-(

NeonMob (2015-2017)

NeonMob was a small (10 people) company that had a card trading platform where you could open packs of digital cards and trade them with others. In addition to the opening and trading, there was also a creation side where much of card content came from. Users could sign up to be creators and get community votes to create a series of cards. These creators made money when people purchased their packs of cards.

I worked on all aspects of the site but my largest project was a friends and messaging system. We would send messages and payloads through Node.js and kept a friends list in MongoDB.

NeonMob was not able to become cash positive quickly enough and the decision was made to move the development overseas. I stayed on to help the overseas team get up to speed and transition the application as smoothly as possible.

Zynga (Intern 2013, fulltime 2014-2015)

I worked as an intern for ChefVille and fulltime on Zynga Poker.

Poker
I worked on the Leagues team for Zynga poker which was/is a feature for leaderboards and seasonal content. You could compare your chip counts and wins against your friends and other people in your League. The top players would recieve bonus chips and gifts to show off their leader status. I worked on both the Facebook side and the (at the time) brand new Unity app that replaced the previous phone applications.

Additionally I took on additional responsibility to update and fix Actionscript bugs in the old Facebook app. No one wanted to do it and I volunteered.

ChefVille (Intern)
Most of my time at ChefVille was bug fixing but at the end I took over a Ruby project that had been left by a previous developer. The project allowed the Zynga artists to put their assets into it to spit out all the different formats and sizes needed for the in game assets. It saved them an incredible amount of time and for taking over the project I was nominated for " Master Chef", an award given each week to the strongest team member. Additionally I recieved a perfect 25/25 on my intern evaluation and was invited back to work fulltime the following year.
